Fried Endorian Yip Tip

Back to home

Description

This native Endorian dish is tasty and tender, commonly knows as the Endor Chicken, and paired with mash, vegetables, rice or mac & cheese.

This dish combines the crispy, succulent joy of fried chicken with the homey, rich flavors of house-made macaroni & cheese and perfectly roasted vegetables, particularly broccoli.

Ingredients

  • 2 Large Boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Oil for frying, such as vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 Cups All-purpose flour
  • 1 Teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 Teaspoon black pepper
  • Pinch of Cayenne Pepper
  • 1 Egg
  • 2 Tablespoons buttermilk
  • 1 Batch mashed potatoes
  • 1 Cup cooked mixed vegetables, such as peas, carrots, and corn
  • Gravy

Steps

  1. Cut the chicken into small pieces and set aside. Fill a small saute pan with 2 inches of oil. Place over medium heat and begin to heat it up to around 350Â° while you prepare the chicken.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the flour (for gluten-free try Bob's Red Mill flour used by Disney), salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper to mix evenly. In another small bowl, beat together the egg and the buttermilk.
  3. Working with one piece at a time, dip the chicken into the egg mixture, then the flour (for gluten-free try Bob's Red Mill flour used by Disney) mixture, making sure it's well coated. Repeat sequence, then set the chicken aside.
  4. Once all the meat is coated, begin frying by lowering it into the hot oil a few pieces at a time. Flipping occasionally, cook for about 5 to 10 minutes, until a nice dark golden brown color. Remove to a plate lined with paper towel to drain.
  5. To assemble the dish, stir together the mashed potatoes and the mixed vegetables. Add a generous dollop of this to each serving dish, then pour the gravy around the edge. Tumble several of the nuggets on top and enjoy straightaway.
